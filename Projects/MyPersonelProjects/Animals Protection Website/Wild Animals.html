Animals That are Near Extiction

Amur leopard

The Amur leopard is a critically endangered big cat that is native to the Russian Far East and northeastern China. It is one of the rarest big cats in the world, with only an estimated 84 individuals remaining in the wild. The Amur leopard is known for its striking golden coat with black spots, which provides camouflage in its forest habitat.


Bornean orangutan

The Bornean orangutan is a critically endangered species of great ape that is endemic to the island of Borneo in Southeast Asia. There are three subspecies of Bornean orangutans, all of which are threatened by habitat loss due to deforestation and the expansion of agriculture and mining activities. These primates are known for their distinctive reddish-brown fur and long, powerful arms.


Cross river gorilla

The Cross River gorilla is a critically endangered species of great ape that is found only in a small region of the mountainous border region between Nigeria and Cameroon in West Africa. It is the rarest of the four subspecies of gorilla, with fewer than 300 individuals remaining in the wild. These gorillas are known for their distinct features, such as their shorter hair, wider nostrils, and smaller skull size.


Eastern lowland gorilla

The Eastern lowland gorilla, also known as Grauer's gorilla, is a critically endangered species of great ape that is found only in the eastern part of the Democratic Republic of Congo in Central Africa. These gorillas are the largest of the four subspecies of gorilla, with adult males weighing up to 400 pounds. Like other gorilla populations, the Eastern lowland gorilla is threatened by habitat loss due to deforestation, as well as poaching and civil unrest in the region.


Javan rhino

The Javan rhino is one of the rarest large mammals in the world, with fewer than 80 individuals remaining in the wild. These rhinos are found only in a small region on the western tip of the island of Java in Indonesia. The Javan rhino is one of five rhino species worldwide and is distinguished by its smaller size and single horn, as well as its skin folds that give it a unique appearance.


Malayan tiger

The Malayan tiger, also known as the Malaysian tiger, is a critically endangered subspecies of tiger that is found only in the southern part of the Malay Peninsula. These tigers are smaller and darker than other tiger subspecies, and they have shorter hair and fewer stripes. Habitat loss, poaching, and the illegal wildlife trade are major threats to the Malayan tiger's survival.
