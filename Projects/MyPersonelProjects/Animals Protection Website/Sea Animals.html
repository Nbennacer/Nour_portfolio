Sea Animals That are Near Extinction

River Dolphins

River dolphins are a group of freshwater dolphins that live in rivers around the world, including the Amazon river and the Ganges river. They are smaller than their ocean-dwelling cousins and have a distinctive long and narrow beak. Many species of river dolphins are endangered due to habitat loss, pollution, and bycatch. To learn more about river dolphins and conservation efforts, click here.


Blue Whale

The blue whale is the largest animal on earth, and can grow up to 100 feet long and weigh as much as 200 tons. Despite their massive size, blue whales feed almost exclusively on tiny shrimp-like animals called krill. These magnificent creatures were hunted nearly to extinction, but are now a protected species. To learn more about blue whales and their conservation, click here.


Giant Devil Ray

The giant devil ray is a type of manta ray that lives in tropical and subtropical oceans around the world. They can grow up to 23 feet across, making them one of the largest fish in the ocean. Unfortunately, giant devil rays are threatened by overfishing and habitat loss. To learn more about giant devil rays and conservation efforts, click here.


Hawksbill Sea Turtle

The hawksbill sea turtle is a critically endangered species of sea turtle that is found in tropical and subtropical oceans around the world. They are named for their distinctive beak-like mouth, which they use to feed on sponges and other small creatures. Hawksbill sea turtles face many threats, including habitat loss, pollution, and bycatch. To learn more about hawksbill sea turtles and conservation efforts, click here.
